Computer-Aided Design (CAD)

What is Computer-Aided Design?

Computer-Aided Design (CAD) is the use of computer systems to assist in the creation, modification, analysis, or optimization of a design. CAD software is used to increase the productivity of the designer, improve the quality of design, improve communications through documentation, and to create a database for manufacturing.

How CAD is Used in Daily Life:
  • Architecture and building design
  • Automotive design and engineering
  • Electronics and circuit design
  • Fashion and textile design
  • Product packaging design
  • Medical device design
  • Furniture and interior design
  • Jewelry design
  • Game and animation design
  • Manufacturing and prototyping

CAD Categories

Vector Graphics

Mathematical representations of images using geometric primitives such as points, lines, curves, and shapes.

  • Scalable without quality loss
  • Perfect for logos and illustrations
  • Small file sizes
  • Precise geometric shapes
Raster Graphics

Digital images composed of a grid of pixels, where each pixel contains color information.

  • Great for photographs and complex images
  • Pixel-based editing
  • Can lose quality when scaled
  • Rich color representation
3D Design

Three-dimensional modeling and design for physical objects, architecture, and virtual environments.

  • Solid modeling
  • Surface modeling
  • Parametric design
  • Assembly modeling
Simulation

Computer simulation of real-world phenomena to test and validate designs before physical implementation.

  • Finite Element Analysis (FEA)
  • Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)
  • Motion simulation
  • Thermal analysis

Tutorial Documentation Structure

You must document your learning and work following this structure so others can follow your process:

  • Week/topic title and what you learned
  • Learning objectives for this week
  • What you want to achieve
  • Software used and versions
  • Time spent learning

  • How you downloaded and installed the software
  • System requirements you encountered
  • Initial settings you configured
  • How you set up your workspace
  • Any plugins or extensions you used

  • What you did step-by-step to learn the software
  • Screenshots of your learning process
  • Settings and parameters you discovered
  • Mistakes you made and how you fixed them
  • Different approaches you tried

  • Files you created during learning
  • Tutorials and guides you followed
  • Reference materials you used
  • Assets or templates you downloaded
  • Your practice files and outputs

  • What you created and screenshots of your work
  • Comparison between different software you tried
  • What worked well and what didn't
  • Key lessons you learned
  • What you want to learn next
