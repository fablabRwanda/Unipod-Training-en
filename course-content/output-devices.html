Overview — Output Devices

Welcome to Output Devices! This week, you'll learn to control actuators and displays—the "hands and voice" of your projects. From LEDs and motors to relays and displays, outputs let your microcontroller interact with the physical world.

We'll explore different types of outputs, learn to drive them safely (especially high-power devices like motors), and create meaningful behaviors. By the end of the week, you'll understand how to choose and control outputs for applications like irrigation systems, safety alarms, educational tools, and more.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand different types of outputs: LEDs, motors, buzzers, relays, displays, etc.
  • Interface at least one power output (e.g. motor or relay) safely.
  • Create simple feedback or actuation behaviors driven by code.

Assignments

  • Individual: Design or reuse a board to drive an output device and demonstrate a meaningful behavior.
  • Group: Document good practices for power, protection (diodes, transistors), and local component sourcing.

Documentation & Evidence

  • Diagrams and code showing how you drive the output device.
  • Photos or videos of the device in action.
  • Short reflection on where such outputs could be useful locally (e.g. irrigation control, safety alarms, educational tools).
