Overview — Networking and Communications

Welcome to Networking and Communications! This week, you'll learn to connect multiple devices together, enabling your projects to share data and work together. From simple serial communication to wireless networks, connectivity opens up powerful possibilities.

We'll explore wired protocols (UART, I2C, SPI) and wireless options (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, LoRa where available). By the end of the week, you'll understand how to build networks of devices that can monitor environments, share data, and coordinate actions—essential for IoT applications in agriculture, health, and infrastructure monitoring.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand basic protocols such as UART, I2C, SPI, and simple wireless options (e.g. Wi‑Fi, Bluetooth, LoRa where available).
  • Implement at least one board‑to‑board or board‑to‑computer communication channel.
  • Visualize or log exchanged data.

Assignments

  • Group: Build and document a simple network of at least two boards exchanging data.
  • Individual: Implement a communication example that fits your local context (e.g. remote sensor, shared display, lab monitoring).

Documentation & Evidence

  • Connection diagrams and code for both ends of the communication.
  • Logs/screenshots showing data successfully sent and received.
  • Notes on range, reliability, and power constraints in your lab or community.
