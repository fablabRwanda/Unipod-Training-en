Overview — Molding and Casting

Welcome to Molding and Casting! This week, you'll learn to create molds and cast parts—a powerful technique for producing multiple copies of objects. Whether you're making soap, resin keychains, or concrete planters, molding and casting enable small-scale production that's perfect for local entrepreneurship.

We'll explore different mold materials (silicone, 3D-printed molds, machined molds) and casting materials (resin, concrete, soap, etc.) that are accessible in African markets. By the end of the week, you'll understand how to design molds, mix and pour casting materials safely, and produce repeatable parts.

Learning Objectives

  • Design positive and negative forms for molding.
  • Understand materials (silicone, urethane, plaster, concrete, etc.) and safety.
  • Produce repeatable cast parts from your mold.

Assignments

  • Individual: Design and mill/print a mold, then cast at least two parts from it.
  • Group: Test different casting materials and document their behavior (strength, surface finish, cure time).

Documentation & Evidence

  • CAD files of your mold design and toolpaths if CNC is used.
  • Photos of mixing, pouring, and demolding steps.
  • Notes on material choices that are realistic for African makers (cost, availability, safety).
