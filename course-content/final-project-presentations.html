Final Project Presentations

Empowering Africa's Future with the UNIPOD Digital Fabrication Program.
In partnership with Timbuktoo, UNDP, FabLab Rwanda and UNIPODS, we're delivering cutting-edge technology education across Africa.

Overview

Welcome to Final Project Presentations! This is the culmination of your UNIPOD journey. You'll present your complete project, demonstrate that it works, and reflect on everything you've learned.

Over the past weeks, you've learned digital fabrication skills, electronics, programming, and design thinking. Now it's time to bring it all together and showcase a complete project that solves a real problem in your community. Your presentation should clearly show the problem you solved, how your system works, and the impact it could have.

This is also a time to reflect on your learning journey and plan how you'll continue using digital fabrication skills after the program. Think about how your project could grow with support from Timbuktoo, UNDP, FabLab Rwanda, or UNIPODS, and how you'll contribute to the growing community of African makers.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Present your project clearly explaining the problem, solution, and impact
  • Demonstrate a working system that integrates all the skills you've learned
  • Document comprehensively so others can understand and reproduce your work
  • Reflect on your learning journey and identify key takeaways
  • Plan next steps for continuing your digital fabrication journey
  • Share knowledge with the UNIPOD community

Presentation Structure

1. Problem Statement

Start by clearly defining the problem:

  • What problem are you solving? Be specific and concrete
  • Who is affected? Define your target users
  • Why is this important? Explain the impact and urgency
  • What is the context? Describe the African context where this applies
  • What are existing solutions? Why aren't they sufficient?

2. Solution Overview

Explain your approach:

  • What is your solution? High-level description
  • How does it work? System architecture and key components
  • What makes it unique? What's different about your approach?
  • Why is it appropriate for your context? How does it fit local needs and constraints?

3. System Details

Show the technical implementation:

  • System diagram: Show all subsystems and connections
  • Key components:
    • Mechanical parts (structure, enclosure, mechanisms)
    • Electronics (sensors, microcontrollers, outputs)
    • Software (code, interfaces, algorithms)
    • Communication (wired, wireless, protocols)
  • Design decisions: Why did you choose these components/approaches?
  • Challenges and solutions: What problems did you face and how did you solve them?

4. Demonstration

Show it working:

  • Live demo: If possible, demonstrate the system in real-time
  • Video demo: If live demo isn't possible, show a recorded demonstration
  • Show main use case: Demonstrate the primary function
  • Show key features: Highlight important capabilities
  • Test results: Show data, measurements, or performance metrics

5. Impact and Future

Discuss the potential:

  • Immediate impact: How does this help users right now?
  • Potential scale: How could this be deployed more widely?
  • Future improvements: What would you add or change?
  • Partnership opportunities: How could Timbuktoo, UNDP, or UNIPODS support this?
  • Business/social model: How could this be sustainable?

6. Reflection

Reflect on your journey:

  • What did you learn? Key skills, concepts, mindsets
  • What was most challenging? What pushed you to grow?
  • What are you most proud of? What achievements stand out?
  • How will you continue? Plans for using these skills after the program
  • How will you contribute? How will you help the UNIPOD community?

Assignments & Documentation Requirements

Final Project Presentation

Create a comprehensive presentation of your final project:

1. Presentation Package (5-10 minutes)
  • Slides covering:
    • Problem statement and context
    • Solution overview
    • System architecture and components
    • Technical implementation details
    • Demonstration
    • Impact and future plans
    • Reflection on learning journey
  • Video or live demo showing:
    • Complete system running
    • Main use case demonstrated
    • Key features highlighted
2. Complete Documentation

Your documentation should include:

  • Problem statement: Clear description of the problem, users, and context
  • System diagram: Complete block diagram showing all subsystems
  • Design files: All CAD files, schematics, PCB layouts
  • Code repository: Complete, well-commented code
  • Bill of materials: Complete list of components with sources and costs
  • Assembly instructions: How to build the system
  • Operating instructions: How to use the system
  • Testing documentation: Test results, performance data
  • License information: How your work is licensed
3. Reflection and Next Steps

Write a reflection covering:

  • Learning journey:
    • What did you learn across all weeks?
    • What tools, skills, and mindsets did you develop?
    • What was most challenging?
    • What are you most proud of?
  • Future plans:
    • How will you continue using digital fabrication?
    • What projects do you want to work on next?
    • How will you contribute to the UNIPOD community?
  • Project growth:
    • How could your project grow with support from Timbuktoo, UNDP, FabLab Rwanda, or UNIPODS?
    • What partnerships or funding opportunities exist?
    • How could this scale to help more people?

Tips for Success

  • Tell a story: Structure your presentation as a narrative—problem → solution → impact → future.
  • Show, don't just tell: Use photos, diagrams, and videos to illustrate your points.
  • Practice your presentation: Rehearse multiple times to ensure smooth delivery.
  • Keep it clear: Avoid jargon, explain technical terms, make it accessible.
  • Highlight the impact: Emphasize how your project helps people and solves real problems.
  • Be honest about challenges: Show what didn't work and how you overcame it—this is valuable learning.
  • Show your process: Include photos of your work in progress, not just final results.
  • Make it reproducible: Include enough detail that others could build on your work.
  • Reflect deeply: Think about what you learned, not just what you made.
  • Think big: Consider how your project could scale and create broader impact.
