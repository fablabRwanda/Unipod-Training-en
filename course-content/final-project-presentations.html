Overview — Final Project Presentations

Welcome to Final Project Presentations! This is the culmination of your UNIPOD journey. You'll present your complete project, demonstrate that it works, and reflect on everything you've learned.

Your presentation should clearly show the problem you solved, how your system works, and the impact it could have in your community. This is also a time to reflect on your learning journey and plan how you'll continue using digital fabrication skills after the program.

What You Must Show

  • Clear problem statement and intended users in your African context.
  • System diagram and summary of key components (mechanics, electronics, code, interface).
  • Live or recorded demo of the project performing its main function.

Presentation Package

  • 5–10 minute talk or video.
  • Slides highlighting process, challenges, and impact.
  • Links to all design files, code, and documentation pages.

Reflection & Next Steps

  • What you learned across all weeks (tools, mindsets, community).
  • How you plan to continue using digital fabrication after the program.
  • How your project could grow with support from Timbuktoo, UNDP, FabLab Rwanda, or UNIPODS.
