Overview — Invention, Intellectual Property, and Income

Welcome to Invention, Intellectual Property, and Income! This week, you'll learn to think about how your projects can become sustainable initiatives or ventures. We'll explore intellectual property options, licensing, and pathways to generate income or social impact.

In African contexts, where innovation often addresses local challenges, understanding IP and business models is crucial. We'll discuss open-source vs. proprietary approaches, simple business models, and how to align your work with goals of organizations like Timbuktoo, UNDP, and UNIPODS.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand basic IP options (copyright, patents, open source, Creative Commons).
  • Decide how you want to license your work and why.
  • Sketch simple business or impact models around your project.

Assignments

  • Individual: Choose and document a license for your final project (with justification).
  • Individual: Draft a short plan for how your project could generate income or social value in your context.

Documentation & Evidence

  • License statement on your project page and in your code/files.
  • 1–2 page write‑up of possible funding, partnerships, or revenue paths (aligned with Timbuktoo/UNDP goals where relevant).
  • Reflection on openness vs. protection and what it means in an African innovation ecosystem.
