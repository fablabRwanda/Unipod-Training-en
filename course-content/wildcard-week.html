Wildcard Week

Empowering Africa's Future with the UNIPOD Digital Fabrication Program.
In partnership with Timbuktoo, UNDP, FabLab Rwanda and UNIPODS, we're delivering cutting-edge technology education across Africa.

Overview

Welcome to Wildcard Week! This is your opportunity to explore a technology or process that isn't fully covered elsewhere in the course. Choose something that interests you, is emerging, or is uniquely relevant to your UNIPOD or community.

This week focuses on four key areas that combine digital fabrication with emerging technologies and real-world challenges:

  • Textile & Wearable Electronics: Combine electronics with fabric to create interactive wearables
  • Virtual Reality (VR): Build virtual prototypes and visualize designs before physical fabrication
  • GreenTech & Green Energy: Tackle renewable energy, recycling, and sustainability challenges
  • Food & AgriTech: Apply technology to agriculture, food production, and preservation

These topics are particularly relevant for African contexts where innovation can address local needs. This week is about deep exploration and creating a tutorial that others can follow. By the end, you'll have documented a new skill or technique that adds to the collective knowledge of African makers. Your work will help build a repository of techniques that are relevant, accessible, and useful in African contexts.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Identify a technology or technique that interests you and is relevant to your context
  • Research and learn a new skill or process independently
  • Experiment and prototype using the new technique
  • Document comprehensively as a tutorial that others can follow
  • Reflect on applications of the technique in African contexts
  • Share knowledge with the UNIPOD community

Wildcard Topics: Textile, VR, GreenTech, Food & AgriTech

This week focuses on four key areas that combine digital fabrication with emerging technologies and real-world challenges. These topics are particularly relevant for African contexts where innovation can address local needs in fashion, visualization, energy, and agriculture.

1. Textile & Wearable Electronics

Combine electronics with fabric to create interactive wearables and smart textiles:

  • Conductive fabrics and threads:
    • Using conductive yarn, fabric, or thread to create circuits
    • LilyPad Arduino and other sewable microcontrollers
    • Creating sensors and actuators embedded in fabric
  • Smart textiles:
    • Temperature-sensitive fabrics
    • LED-integrated clothing
    • Haptic feedback in garments
    • Motion sensors in fabric
  • Digital textile printing:
    • Using vinyl cutters for fabric appliqué
    • Heat transfer techniques
    • Custom textile patterns from digital designs
  • Applications in African contexts:
    • Safety clothing with integrated lights for visibility
    • Health monitoring wearables using local materials
    • Educational toys that combine traditional textiles with electronics
    • Fashion tech for local entrepreneurship
  • Tools and materials:
    • LilyPad Arduino or Adafruit Flora
    • Conductive thread, fabric, or tape
    • LEDs, sensors, small actuators
    • Traditional sewing tools and local fabrics

2. Virtual Reality (VR) for Prototyping

Build virtual prototypes in VR to visualize and test designs before physical fabrication:

  • VR design and visualization:
    • Creating 3D models in VR environments (Gravity Sketch, Tilt Brush, Oculus Medium)
    • Importing CAD models into VR for review
    • Virtual prototyping before physical creation
  • VR for training and education:
    • Teaching digital fabrication processes in VR
    • Simulating machine operations safely
    • Collaborative design sessions in virtual spaces
  • VR interfaces for hardware:
    • Controlling physical devices through VR
    • Visualizing sensor data in VR
    • Remote operation of machines via VR
  • Applications in African contexts:
    • Remote training for UNIPOD students across locations
    • Visualizing architectural or product designs before building
    • Educational experiences that don't require physical materials
    • Collaborative design with teams across Africa
  • Tools and platforms:
    • Oculus Quest, HTC Vive, or smartphone VR (Cardboard, Gear VR)
    • Gravity Sketch, Tilt Brush, Blender (with VR add-ons)
    • Unity or Unreal Engine for custom VR experiences
    • WebXR for browser-based VR (no headset required)

3. GreenTech & Green Energy

Tackle real-world challenges in renewable energy, recycling, and sustainability:

  • Renewable energy systems:
    • Solar panel installations and monitoring
    • Wind turbine design and fabrication
    • Small-scale hydroelectric systems
    • Battery storage and management systems
  • Energy monitoring and efficiency:
    • Building energy monitoring systems
    • Smart lighting and power management
    • IoT sensors for energy tracking
    • Optimizing energy use in UNIPOD labs
  • Recycling and upcycling:
    • Plastic recycling systems (shredders, extruders for 3D printing)
    • E-waste repurposing (using old electronics components)
    • Creating new materials from waste
    • Designing for circular economy
  • Sustainable materials:
    • Bioplastics from local plant materials
    • Natural fiber composites (sisal, jute, bamboo)
    • Mycelium-based materials
    • Local material sourcing and processing
  • Applications in African contexts:
    • Off-grid solar solutions for rural communities
    • Affordable renewable energy systems
    • Local waste management solutions
    • Sustainable building materials from local resources
    • Energy-efficient cooking and heating solutions
  • Tools and components:
    • Solar panels, charge controllers, batteries
    • Microcontrollers for monitoring (ESP32, Arduino)
    • Sensors (voltage, current, temperature)
    • 3D printing for custom mounts and enclosures

4. Food & AgriTech

Apply digital fabrication and electronics to food production and agriculture challenges:

  • Precision agriculture:
    • Soil moisture sensors and automated irrigation
    • Weather monitoring stations
    • Plant health monitoring using sensors
    • Automated greenhouse systems
  • Automated farming equipment:
    • Simple robots for planting, weeding, or harvesting
    • Automated feeders for livestock
    • Custom tools fabricated with digital methods
  • Food processing and preservation:
    • Solar dehydrators for food preservation
    • Custom molds for food production
    • Packaging solutions using digital fabrication
    • Temperature and humidity monitoring for storage
  • Aquaponics and hydroponics:
    • Automated nutrient delivery systems
    • pH and nutrient sensors
    • Water level and flow monitoring
    • Custom growing structures (3D printed, laser cut)
  • Applications in African contexts:
    • Improving crop yields with affordable sensor systems
    • Reducing post-harvest losses with monitoring and preservation
    • Small-scale automated systems for family farms
    • Food security solutions using local resources
    • Urban farming systems for cities
  • Tools and components:
    • Microcontrollers (Arduino, ESP32) for automation
    • Sensors (moisture, temperature, pH, light, humidity)
    • Actuators (pumps, valves, motors)
    • 3D printing for custom mounts and enclosures
    • Laser cutting for precision parts

Mini-Lab: Choose and Explore Your Wildcard Topic

Let's structure your wildcard exploration focusing on Textile, VR, GreenTech, or Food & AgriTech to ensure you create valuable documentation.

Step 1: Choose Your Topic Area

Select one of the four focus areas that:

  • Interests you personally
  • Addresses a real need in your community
  • Is feasible with available resources in your UNIPOD
  • Can be explored and documented in one week
  • Adds value to the UNIPOD community knowledge base

Focus Areas:

  • Textile & Wearable Electronics: Create interactive clothing, accessories, or textile-based sensors
  • VR for Prototyping: Use VR to design, visualize, or prototype solutions
  • GreenTech & Green Energy: Work on renewable energy, recycling, or sustainability projects
  • Food & AgriTech: Apply technology to agriculture, food production, or preservation challenges

Step 2: Research

Gather information:

  • Find existing tutorials, papers, or documentation
  • Identify required materials and tools
  • Understand safety considerations
  • Find examples or case studies

Step 3: Plan Your Experiment

Define what you'll do:

  • What specific experiment or prototype will you create?
  • What materials and tools do you need?
  • What are the key steps?
  • What will you measure or test?

Step 4: Execute and Document

As you work:

  • Take photos at each step
  • Record settings, parameters, and measurements
  • Note what works and what doesn't
  • Document failures and how you fixed them

Step 5: Create Tutorial

Structure your documentation as a tutorial:

  • Introduction: What is this technique? Why is it useful?
  • Materials and tools: Complete list with sources
  • Step-by-step process: Clear instructions with photos
  • Parameters and settings: Exact values used
  • Results: What you achieved, photos of outcomes
  • Lessons learned: What worked, what didn't, improvements
  • Applications: How could this be used in African contexts?

Assignments & Documentation Requirements

Individual or Group Assignment

Choose a wildcard topic, run at least one experiment/prototype, and document it as a tutorial others can follow:

  • Select a topic that:
    • Isn't fully covered in other weeks
    • Interests you and is relevant to your context
    • Is feasible with available resources
  • Run at least one experiment or create one prototype
  • Document the process comprehensively as a tutorial
  • Reflect on how this technique could serve African makers

Documentation Checklist

Item Description
Introduction and Motivation Clear explanation of what the technique is, why it's useful, and why you chose it. Include context about relevance to African makers.
Materials and Tools Complete list of all materials and tools needed, with:
  • Specific names and part numbers
  • Quantities required
  • Where to source (especially local sources)
  • Approximate costs
Step-by-Step Process Detailed instructions with:
  • Clear step numbering
  • Photos at each major step
  • Warnings for safety-critical steps
  • Troubleshooting tips
Parameters and Settings Document all important parameters:
  • Exact settings used (temperatures, speeds, times, etc.)
  • File parameters (if using digital tools)
  • Any calculations or formulas
Results Show what you achieved:
  • Photos of final results
  • Measurements or test results
  • Comparison with expectations
Files and Resources Share all files others can reuse:
  • CAD files, code, configuration files
  • Links to external resources
  • References to papers or tutorials you used
Lessons Learned Reflection on:
  • What worked well
  • What didn't work and why
  • What you would do differently
  • Common mistakes to avoid
Applications for African Makers Reflection on:
  • How could this technique be used in African contexts?
  • What problems could it solve?
  • What adaptations might be needed?
  • What business or social impact opportunities exist?
Remember: Your tutorial should be clear enough that someone else in another UNIPOD could follow it and reproduce your work. Include enough detail but keep it readable and well-organized.

Tips for Success

  • Choose something achievable: Pick a topic you can realistically explore and document in one week.
  • Start with research: Learn from existing resources before experimenting.
  • Document as you go: Take photos and notes during the process, not after.
  • Test and iterate: Don't expect perfection on the first try—document your learning process.
  • Think locally: Consider how the technique could be adapted for local materials, conditions, and needs.
  • Share knowledge: Your tutorial helps build the collective knowledge of African makers.
  • Be thorough: Include enough detail that others can reproduce your work.
  • Reflect on impact: Think about how this technique could create value in your community.
