Overview — Computer-Controlled Machining

Welcome to Computer-Controlled Machining! This week, you'll learn to use large-format CNC routers to create furniture, signage, and other big objects. CNC machining enables you to work with full sheets of plywood, MDF, or other materials, creating strong, precise assemblies.

We'll focus on designing for CNC (press-fit joints, toolpath considerations), using CAM software to generate safe G-code, and operating large machines safely. By the end of the week, you'll have created a substantial object that addresses a local need—whether it's classroom furniture, lab storage, or community signage.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand toolpaths, feeds & speeds, and material holding for CNC routers.
  • Design a parametric 2.5D object suitable for plywood or similar sheet materials.
  • Use CAM software to generate safe G‑code for your lab’s machine.

Assignments

  • Group: Characterize your CNC (run test cuts for different tools, feeds, and materials, and document results).
  • Individual: Design and fabricate a big object that uses press‑fit or mechanical joints.
  • Individual: Reflect on how your design addresses a local need (e.g. low‑cost classroom furniture, lab storage).

Documentation & Evidence

  • Design files (CAD + CAM) and exported toolpaths.
  • Photos of machining, assembly, and final object in use.
  • Notes on settings, tool choices, and what you would change next time.
