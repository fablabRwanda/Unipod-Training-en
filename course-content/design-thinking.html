Design Thinking for African Makers

Empowering Africa's Future with the UNIPOD Digital Fabrication Program.
In partnership with Timbuktoo, UNDP, FabLab Rwanda and UNIPODS, we're delivering cutting-edge technology education across Africa.

From Community Challenges to Prototypes

This week introduces design thinking—a human-centered approach to innovation that starts with understanding people's needs and ends with solutions that are desirable, feasible, and viable. You will learn to frame problems in your community, prototype quickly in the lab, and document your process so others can build on your ideas.

Learning Objectives

  • Use design thinking stages: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, test.
  • Map stakeholders and constraints for an African context (access, cost, environment).
  • Translate concepts into low‑fidelity prototypes using UNIPOD tools.

Assignments

  • Individual: Document at least one problem from your community and sketch three potential solutions.
  • Group: Choose one idea and build a quick physical prototype (paper, cardboard, laser‑cut, or 3D printed).
  • Individual: Reflect on user feedback and how you would iterate in later technical weeks.
